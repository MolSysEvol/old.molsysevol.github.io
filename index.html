This is the homepage of the
Research Group Molecular Systems Evolution

Our research aims at understanding the evolution of genomes. By comparing genome sequences from various individuals and/or species, one can gain insights into the process of how and when species are created, a fundamental aspect of life and, therefore, of biology. In order to do so, we combine modeling approaches and statistical data analysis, as well as lab experiments when possible. Our research makes us wander in the fields of phylogenetics, population genomics and structural biology, as well as systems biology. In particular, we are interested in characterising stochastic processes at distinct levels of organization, in order to understand their role in evolution.

Molecular Coevolution

The study of coevolution at the molecular level.

Population Genomics Modeling

Developing models of genome evolution in populations, within the sequentially Markov coalescent framework.

Evolutionary Models of Gene Networks

New models of gene networks in population, with an inheritable component of expression noise

Population Genomics of Fungi

Infering the demographic history and patterns of selection in fungal (pathogen) genomes.

Adaptive Evolution

Studying the patterns of adaptive substitutions between populations, withing genomes and genes, and across time.

Data and Software

Data and Software developed and maintained by the lab, all open access and open source.
